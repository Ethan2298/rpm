# Common Objections and Responses

## "The price is too high"
- Acknowledge it. Don't dismiss.
- Ask what their budget range is — this tells you if they're close or way off.
- If close: highlight what makes this unit special (mileage, condition, rarity).
- If way off: suggest alternatives in their range rather than pushing.

## "I need to think about it"
- Totally fine. Don't push.
- Ask if there's anything specific they're unsure about so you can get them info.
- Set a follow-up: "No rush — mind if I check back in a couple days?"

## "I found it cheaper somewhere else"
- Ask where and what the details are (mileage, condition, history).
- Often the "cheaper" one has higher mileage, accident history, or missing features.
- If theirs is genuinely better, be honest. Builds trust for the next one.

## "I'm not ready to buy yet"
- Find out their timeline. "When are you thinking?"
- If it's weeks, add them to a nurture sequence with relevant inventory updates.
- If it's months, tag them and check back quarterly.

## "Can you do financing?"
- Confirm what financing options are available at the dealership.
- If yes, connect them with the finance person.
- If not, suggest they get pre-approved through their bank or credit union.
